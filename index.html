TDF Project: AI-based ChatBOT in Programming Education

Research Assistant

Supervisor: Erick Purwanto [Erick.Purwanto@xjtlu.edu.cn]; Yihong Wang [Yihong.Wang@xjtlu.edu.cn]; Thomas Selig [Thomas.Selig@xjtlu.edu.cn]

  1. Phase One: Created a programming tutor bot based on open-source projects tailored for pair programming, winning the second prize at the XJTLU Student Research-Oriented Learning Summit

    • Utilized Prompt Engineering to call OpenAI APIs, creating an educational chatbot for programming. Implemented Chain of Thought and Moderation to prevent non-educational output, making it suitable for beginners.
    • Tested the bot with an Indonesian student to verify its effectiveness and multilingual capabilities.

  2. Phase Two: Developed algorithm visualization module based on bot responses to aid teaching.

    • Created seven algorithm visualizations using d3.node, including for loops and array sorting lessons. Modified previous React-based TypeScript project to enable response-driven visualizations. Developed a static website with embedded chatbot to package programming knowledge and LLM into courses.
    • Conducted 2 controlled experiments with pre-A-level students and 2 controlled experiments with 120 non-programming background students from the CPT206 course at XJTLU, demonstrating improved learning efficiency with the chatbot. Preparing a publication based on these findings and secured Advance HE Global Impact Grants 2023-24.

  3. Phase Three: Further research on the effectiveness of our design compared to standard GPT in teaching graph algorithms.

    • Iterated three graph algorithm visualizations using d3.force, including DFS-based cycle and connectivity checks, and BFS-based connected components check. Added data recording and user-friendly guidance modules.
    • Conducted 8 controlled experiments to verify its effectiveness: experiments involved 330 students with varying programming backgrounds from the CPT204 course at XJTLU. One group used our bot for the set learning tasks, while the other group used AI based on the original GPT. The results showed a significant improvement in learning efficiency for students using the chatbot. The project was showcased at EdVenture and internal sharing seminars.
